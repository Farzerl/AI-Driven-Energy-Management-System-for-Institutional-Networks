# Measurement and Verification Plan

## Objective

Verify whether forecast-assisted operational scheduling reduces maximum demand and peak-period consumption without affecting critical services.

## Pilot phases

### Phase 1 - Baseline

Duration: four weeks.

Record:

- half-hour energy;
- maximum kVA demand;
- tariff period;
- facility operating schedule;
- outages and abnormal events;
- operator actions;
- power factor where available.

### Phase 2 - Advisory operation

Duration: four to eight weeks.

The system produces recommendations. Estates and Facilities confirms, defers or dismisses each action. No autonomous switching is used.

### Phase 3 - Comparison

Compare matched weekdays and operating conditions using:

- monthly maximum demand;
- peak-period kWh;
- standard and off-peak kWh;
- high-risk peaks warned;
- accepted recommendations;
- unnecessary recommendations;
- operator response time;
- data availability;
- service-impact incidents.

## Financial verification

The authorised institutional tariff is applied only inside the controlled UZ environment. Public reporting should contain rounded totals, percentage savings and anonymised results.

## Acceptance thresholds

A pilot may be considered promising when it demonstrates:

- no impact on critical loads;
- at least 95% data availability;
- a repeatable reduction in matched peak demand;
- documented operator acceptance;
- savings exceeding recurring system operating cost.

No threshold is a guarantee. Final acceptance remains an Estates, Finance and engineering decision.
