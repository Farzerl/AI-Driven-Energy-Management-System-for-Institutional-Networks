# Public-Source Tariff and Cost-Impact Methodology

## Purpose

This evidence estimates the order of magnitude of campus electricity expenditure and the potential value of peak management without disclosing the University of Zimbabwe account tariff, bill or customer information.

The result is a planning model, not an invoice reconstruction.

## Public tariff anchor

Zimbabwe's National Energy Compact, published by the Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority, reports an average end-user electricity tariff of 16.08 US cents per kWh. It states that a 21% adjustment was implemented on 1 December 2023 to reach that level.

ZERA's public home page also displayed USD 4.24 for the first 50 domestic units as at 8 July 2026. This equals USD 0.0848/kWh and is used only as a reasonableness check for a subsidised domestic block. It is not used as the institutional tariff.

## Study figures used

The study provides monthly campus consumption and load factors from September 2025 to April 2026. It also provides tariff-component shares for January 2026 and April 2026.

January 2026 cost shares:

- Peak energy: 45.5%
- Standard energy: 28.0%
- Off-peak energy: 11.5%
- Demand: 11.0%
- Reactive energy: 4.0%

April 2026 cost shares:

- Peak energy: 51.2%
- Standard energy: 24.3%
- Off-peak energy: 8.7%
- Demand: 12.0%
- Reactive energy: 3.8%

These figures show that peak energy and maximum demand are major cost drivers.

## Baseline bill estimate

For each month:

`estimated bill = monthly energy (kWh) x USD 0.1608/kWh`

This uses the public national average tariff as an effective blended rate. It avoids pretending that the private UZ tariff is known.

## Maximum demand estimate

The monthly maximum demand proxy is derived from the study's energy and load-factor figures:

`estimated maximum demand = monthly energy / (hours in month x load factor)`

The result is treated as an apparent-demand planning proxy in kVA because the study discusses maximum-demand billing in kVA. A utility demand-register export would be required for billing-grade verification.

## Equivalent planning rates

The scenario model uses equivalent public-source planning rates:

- Peak energy: USD 0.2173/kWh
- Standard energy: USD 0.1150/kWh
- Off-peak energy: USD 0.0588/kWh
- Demand: USD 7.78/kVA-month

These are not published ZETDC account rates. They are calibrated proxies derived from the public average tariff and the January/April cost composition in the study.

## Scenario interpretation

The three scenarios represent operational planning assumptions:

- Conservative: 3% monthly peak reduction and 1% of energy shifted from peak to standard.
- Central planning: 5% monthly peak reduction, 2% shifted from peak to standard and 0.5% shifted from peak to off-peak.
- Stretch planning: 8% monthly peak reduction, 3% shifted from peak to standard and 1% shifted from peak to off-peak.

The central scenario estimates approximately USD 14,170 across the eight-month study period, or 2.35% of the estimated baseline bill. This is not a verified saving claim.

## Claim boundary

The model does not include taxes, fixed charges, account-specific adjustments, meter multipliers, loss factors, arrears, negotiated rates or actual reactive-energy billing rules. It must be replaced with the applicable tariff and demand register during the pilot measurement and verification stage.
