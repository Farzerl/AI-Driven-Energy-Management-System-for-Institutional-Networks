# Tariff Estimation Basis and Privacy Position

## Why a public estimate is used

The University of Zimbabwe electricity tariff documents and bills are not published in this repository. The submission therefore uses public regulatory information and aggregate figures already reported in the institutional electricity study.

This protects:

- customer account numbers;
- negotiated or account-specific tariff terms;
- exact billed amounts;
- meter identifiers;
- taxes, arrears and payment information;
- confidential institutional operating data.

## Evidence hierarchy

1. Public ZERA national average tariff: 16.08 USc/kWh.
2. Study monthly energy and load-factor figures.
3. Study January and April tariff-component proportions.
4. Public planning assumptions for peak reduction and load shifting.
5. Future pilot verification against authorised billing and demand-register data.

## Correct wording for the proposal and pitch

Use:

> Applying the public ZERA average end-user tariff of 16.08 USc/kWh to the study's aggregate campus consumption gives an indicative eight-month expenditure of approximately USD 604,000. A central peak-management scenario indicates an order-of-magnitude saving of approximately USD 14,000, subject to tariff, meter and controllable-load verification during the pilot.

Do not use:

> The University paid exactly USD 604,000.

Do not use:

> The system has already saved USD 14,000.

## Update requirement

The model must be updated when an authorised UZ representative confirms the applicable billing structure. The confidential source document should remain outside the public repository.
